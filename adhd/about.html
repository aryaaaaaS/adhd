Understanding ADHD

Learn about Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder and how FocusCheck can help with early detection

What is ADHD?

Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) is a neurodevelopmental disorder affecting both children and adults. It is characterized by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with functioning and development.

ADHD typically begins in childhood and can continue through adolescence and into adulthood. Children with ADHD may struggle with paying attention, controlling impulsive behaviors, or be overly active.

Early detection and intervention can significantly improve outcomes for children with ADHD, helping them to develop coping strategies and receive appropriate support both at home and in educational settings.

FocusCheck: AI-Powered ADHD Detection

FocusCheck is an innovative AI-based tool designed to assist in the early detection of ADHD symptoms in children. Our project combines cutting-edge artificial intelligence with established psychological assessment methods to provide a comprehensive screening tool for parents, educators, and healthcare providers.

How ADHD Detection Works

FocusCheck uses a multi-modal approach to assess potential ADHD symptoms:

  • Behavior analysis through video recordings
  • Attention tracking during interactive tasks
  • Machine learning algorithms that compare results to established patterns
  • Parent and teacher questionnaires for comprehensive evaluation

Our AI system analyzes these inputs to provide a preliminary assessment that can help guide further professional evaluation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions parents have about ADHD and our assessment process

How early can ADHD be detected in children?

While ADHD is most commonly diagnosed in school-age children (typically between 6-12 years old), symptoms can often be observed in children as young as 3-4 years old. Early signs may include excessive motor activity, difficulty following instructions, and problems with self-regulation. FocusCheck is designed to help identify potential signs from preschool age, although a clinical diagnosis typically comes later.

Is FocusCheck a replacement for professional diagnosis?

No, FocusCheck is not a diagnostic tool and cannot replace professional clinical assessment. Our system is designed as a screening tool to help identify children who may benefit from further evaluation by qualified healthcare professionals such as pediatricians, child psychologists, or psychiatrists. Think of FocusCheck as a first step that can provide valuable information to discuss with medical professionals.

What are the common symptoms of ADHD in children?

ADHD symptoms generally fall into three categories:

  • Inattention: Difficulty sustaining attention, easily distracted, forgetful, loses things, makes careless mistakes
  • Hyperactivity: Fidgeting, running or climbing excessively, difficulty staying seated, talks excessively
  • Impulsivity: Interrupting others, difficulty waiting turn, blurting out answers

It's important to note that children may predominantly show symptoms from one category or a combination of symptoms from different categories.

How does FocusCheck protect my child's privacy?

We take privacy very seriously, especially when it comes to children's data. FocusCheck employs end-to-end encryption for all video assessments, and all data is stored in compliance with HIPAA and GDPR regulations. Video data is processed locally when possible and automatically deleted after analysis unless explicitly saved by parents. Parents maintain complete control over all data and can request deletion at any time. We never share individual assessment data with third parties without explicit consent.

My child is very active but can focus on things they enjoy. Could they still have ADHD?

Yes, this is actually a common characteristic of many children with ADHD. The ability to hyperfocus on highly interesting activities while struggling to maintain attention on less stimulating tasks is a pattern often seen in ADHD. This contradiction can sometimes lead to misunderstandings where parents or teachers think a child is "choosing" not to pay attention. FocusCheck's assessment includes tasks with varying levels of engagement to help identify these patterns.
